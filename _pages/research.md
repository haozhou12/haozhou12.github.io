My research studies privacy, security, and trustworthiness in distributed and intelligent learning systems. I am interested in both theoretical mechanisms and practical systems for secure AI.

## AI Security

I study security risks in AI systems, including privacy-preserving fine-tuning, backdoor attacks and defenses, federated distillation, and security testing for large models.

## Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning

I work on efficient and robust federated learning frameworks for dynamic edge environments, with a focus on client dynamics, heterogeneous devices, communication efficiency, and privacy protection.

## Differential Privacy

I study differential privacy mechanisms for federated learning, including adaptive gradient clipping, personalized differential privacy, and privacy-utility optimization under heterogeneous and multimodal data.

## Verifiable Federated Learning

I build verifiable federated learning frameworks by combining cryptographic tools and biometric authentication, aiming to provide integrity, traceability, and trustworthiness in federated aggregation.

## Emerging Directions

I am also exploring security issues in large language models, federated agents, and collaborative inference systems involving large and small models.
